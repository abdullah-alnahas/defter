Reading slowly is not a failure of speed. It is a different mode of attention,
one that lets the sentence breathe and the meaning settle.

A book read in a single afternoon may pass through the eyes; a book read over
weeks passes through the mind.

## Three habits

- Stop at unfamiliar words. Look them up.
- Re-read paragraphs that surprised you.
- Keep a notebook nearby — not to summarize, but to argue.
